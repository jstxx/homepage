About Me

I've spent over 20 years building technology that’s meant to push boundaries and make things better. I started with Nexus, a network of digital gaming centers I founded from scratch. I built the machines myself—bare hands, custom rigs—and learned quickly what scalability and uptime really mean when you've got a room full of people depending on your setup. It wasn’t flashy. It was raw, hands-on work, but it’s where I realized that solving hard problems with tech is what I’m meant to do.

Later, I worked with Apple, helping build out Apple Retail’s technology infrastructure. Apple taught me the importance of refinement—not just making things function, but making them feel invisible when they work perfectly. Simplicity at scale. That lesson has stuck with me ever since.

Since then, I’ve worked with teams at places like Amazon, One Medical, Condé Nast, and Citizen, tackling complex systems and real-world challenges. At One Medical, I built treatment workflows that now handle hundreds of thousands of interactions each month. At Citizen, I worked on core app features that scaled to 20 million downloads. And at The New Yorker Today, I led efforts to drive secure, scalable subscription features that helped generate over $150M in revenue.

I specialize in mobile development, platform scalability, and cloud services, but I don’t get caught up in buzzwords. For me, it's about creating tech that’s fast, solid, and gets out of the way. I like systems that don’t break under pressure. I like working with teams who care about getting it right—whether that’s optimizing performance, ensuring accessibility, or leading cross-functional projects to deliver something real.

I've also built platforms like EquitySlice, a tool that simplifies equity calculations for users, and Moola!, a financial management platform built on live market APIs and machine learning. Both were born out of a need to streamline complicated systems, breaking them down into something people can actually use and trust.

At the end of the day, I'm about building. Fixing. Scaling. Tearing down what's unnecessary and leaving behind tech that lasts. Whether it’s designing infrastructure for a product that millions rely on or launching new platforms, I’m here to make the impossible feel inevitable.
